Government Markets

Colin White

A recent Jeramey Jannene article at Urban Milwaukee titled New Deconstruction Firm Makes Progress observes the current state of a 2018 deconstruction requirement put in place by the Milwaukee Common Council.

Instead of mechanically smashing old buildings and sending the materials to a landfill, the structures should be dissembled by entry-level workers and the salvageable materials, notably old-growth lumber, sold to contractors and home-improvement stores. The revenue from the materials would offset the cost of the increased amount of labor, with jobs being created in the process.

This deconstruction requirement has been suspended each year of its existence and has only recently found a company that is able to fit all of the City's requirements and is willing to do the work.

The ordinance requires a contractor on a city-owned deconstruction job to use a certified, disadvantaged Small Business Enterprise, which White Glove itself is, and hire unemployed or underemployed city residents through the Residents Preference Program (RPP). The DNS supervisor said approximately 60% of project work hours on White Glove’s jobs were completed by RPP-eligible workers.

I am a big admirer of the idea of salvaging the best of old buildings for use in other projects. I have a hard time believing that our government is the best way to create a market and have it thrive. The reason most people demolish rather than deconstruct is because it is significantly cheaper to do. When you add requirements on who a business is allowed to hire and add certification requirements on top of that it simply adds additional burden and cost and leads to nobody being left to perform the task.

Government is incapable of resolving the problems that continue to harm our community. Resolving persistent poverty, crime and unemployment is not part of its purpose. Government exists to operate in the background and protect individual's inherent right to improve their lives by ensuring equal access to opportunity.

Property owners should be allowed to do what they wish with their homes whether building, demolishing or deconstructing as long as they are not encroaching on other's rights.
